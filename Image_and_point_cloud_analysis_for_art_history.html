Image and point cloud analysis for art history

    Supervisor: Isabelle Bloch (LIP6), Dominique Béréziat (LIP6), researchers at Plemo3D platform
    Contact: isabelle.bloch@sorbonne-universite.fr

    Description:

    The Plemo3D platform of Sorbonne Université is involved in the acquisition and analysis of historical objects, at various scales (from small coins to large buildings), to suppose studies in art history. The acquired data can be either 3D point clouds or color images. The aim of this project is to develop computational methods to help analyzing these data. The project can take several directions, depending on the interest of the students and the availability of data. Examples include:
    • suppression of reflections and shadows
    • segment objects of interest from background and surrounding objects
    • recognition of objects for automatic classification
    • recognizing colors in Japanese prints to help in their datation
    • recognizing objects (e.g. dragons on Japanese sculptures) to help identifying the sculptor
    • register several partial acquisitions of an object to build a complete object
    Work:
    • 1. Literature review and identification of relevant methods to solve one of the above problems.
    • 2. Development and implementation of a method. Test on real data from the Plemo3D platform.
