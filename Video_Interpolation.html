Video interpolation

    Supervisor: Dominique Béréziat
    Contact: dominique.bereziat@lip6.fr

    Description:

    Video interpolation is a technique used to generate intermediate frames between consecutive frames in a video sequence. The primary goal is to enhance the temporal resolution, reduce motion blur, and create smoother transitions between frames. This task is crucial in many applications, specially in climate change modeling. Temporal interpolation can be based on optical flow, but here we propose to study other methods that do not rely on optical flow. For instance, in (Harilal et al; 2023) is proposed self-supervised method using multiple cycle of consistency losses.
    Tasks to achieve:
    • review literature in video interpolation
    • implement [1]
    • experiment on natural video sequence and geophysical fields, and compare to a baseline (persistence, bicubic, ...)

    References:
    [1] Harilal, R., Venkataraman, A., & Babu, R. V. (2023). Self-supervised video interpolation using cycle consistency. In Proceedings of the IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (pp. 20809-20818).
